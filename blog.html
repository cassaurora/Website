Growing up in Northwestern Ontario, I spent much of my summers at camp. Most camps had sauna's since there happens to be a large settler Finnish community back home. Sauna's became part of a quintessential summer experience. Much like the Fins, many other Indigenous cultures around the world have their own version of the sauna, including First Nations in the form of a Sweatlodge.

To me, sauna experiences represent a form of ceremony.

So, since moving to Whistler, B.C. I was missing both experiences. Which ultimately led me to a program led by Whistler Centre for Sustainability. In November 2019, I was announced as the winner of the Tourism Start-Up! Since then, I have embarked on launching a Mobile Barrel Sauna Rental business.

Sauna's are safe, shared spaces which embody natural wellness and community. An opportunity to disconnect from devices and connect within or with friends and family. A chance to integrate fire and water surrounded in sacred cedar.

Our Indigenous designed saunas plan to offer a luxury "wellness on wheels" service available in Whistler BC, a year-round destination resort bringing exclusive relaxation surrounded by nature and mountains.

In a mountain town that hosts over 3 million guests per year, guests can discover an opportunity to engage in self-care, while being able to socialize and have the flexibility to take time utilizing wellness services either solo or with friends or family of their choice anytime year-round.

There are many health benefits of saunas including improved cardiovascular performance, aid in sports recovery, flush toxins, reduces incidences of Alzheimer's by 65%, induces improved deeper sleep, burns calories and relieves stress.

Tourists, including event planners, those involved in adventure sports and mountain enthusiasts, who are looking for rejuvenation and authentic experiences in Whistler can rent a traditional wood-burning or infrared 10 person sauna. Rentals include convenient and hassle-free delivery and pick-up services. Each sauna is muraled in authentic Indigenous art stationed on a trailer and equipped with complimentary towels, bathrobes and Indigenous aroma therapies. An experience guests can have again and again.
